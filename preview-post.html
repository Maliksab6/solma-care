The PCOS Diet Advice That Ignores How We Actually Eat

By Salma Tabbsum · Jan 15, 2025 · 8 min read

Quinoa and kale aren't the answer when your family meals are built around rice, roti, and dal. Here's what actually works for South Asian women with PCOS.

Why Standard PCOS Diet Advice Doesn't Work

If you've Googled "PCOS diet" you've seen the same advice repeated everywhere: eat more leafy greens, swap rice for quinoa, add protein to every meal. This advice isn't wrong — it's just built for a completely different food culture.

For South Asian women, the problem isn't that we don't know how to eat. It's that the dietary frameworks we're given assume a Western plate. Our meals are structured differently — rice is a staple, not a treat. Roti is comfort, not a carb to avoid.

The Real Issue: Insulin Resistance in South Asian Bodies

South Asian women have a higher genetic predisposition to insulin resistance. This means our bodies process carbohydrates differently — and the standard low-carb advice often backfires because it doesn't account for:

  • Cultural food patterns that are deeply ingrained
  • The social pressure around food at family gatherings
  • The psychological impact of restricting foods your family eats daily
"When your doctor tells you to 'cut carbs' but your grandmother's dal rice is the most nutritious thing in your kitchen — that's not medical advice. That's cultural amnesia."

What Actually Works

The approach that works for South Asian women with PCOS isn't about eliminating foods — it's about understanding how to combine them. Here's the framework that makes a real difference:

Start with what's already on your plate. Add protein and healthy fats before the rice. Eat the salad or sabzi first. This simple change — without removing anything from your diet — can significantly improve insulin sensitivity.
